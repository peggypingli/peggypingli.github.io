# Communicate Like An Artist

[cite_start]Escalation/Situation Management requires a high level of communication skills[cite: 2]. [cite_start]I found my life can get easier in any area of my life/work circles if I practice what I learned in the last 20 years of problem management work[cite: 6]. 

[cite_start]I use the Artist as an analogy for the reason below [cite: 8][cite_start]: The goal of an artist is to deliver a message or an impression in one artwork[cite: 9]. [cite_start]To reach it, the artist needs to keep the message in mind and carefully decides what to paint/design[cite: 10]. [cite_start]The communication aims for the similar goal - information transmission[cite: 12]. [cite_start]Good communication should be efficient and right to the point (no more, no less)[cite: 13]. 

[cite_start]Here is my experience on how to make communication efficient[cite: 14].

## Research the WHY to plan your communication
[cite_start]When managing escalations, our role expects us to be the communication owner[cite: 16]. [cite_start]To meet this, we need to understand the following as a start point[cite: 17]:
* [cite_start]Why is the situation escalated? [cite: 18]
* [cite_start]Do we know whose fault yet? [cite: 19]
* Who are my audiences? [cite_start]Do they have any particular communication styles? [cite: 20]
* [cite_start]What is in it for my audience? [cite: 21]
* [cite_start]What is the desired outcome? [cite: 22]

[cite_start]Through this research, we aim to understand the escalated situation more so we can define what approaches/responses are required for the given situation[cite: 23]. [cite_start]Even though every situation is different, the Cynefin Framework summarizes any situation into 4 basic categories: Obvious, Complicated, Complex and Chaotic[cite: 25]. [cite_start]This can be used to structure the approach including our communication approach to finding the correct solution that matches the given conditions of the situation[cite: 26].

[cite_start]In Chinese, we have a proverb - 三思而后行[cite: 27]. [cite_start]It means - think three times before you act[cite: 27]. [cite_start]This WHY research is the most important part of any communication[cite: 28]. [cite_start]We need to focus on the WHY along the whole communication journey[cite: 29]. [cite_start]This will help us avoid the side distractions by emotions, self-defense and bias during crucial conversations[cite: 30].

## Identify the WHAT to determine your messages
[cite_start]Stay unbiased when generating your messages: you are here to convey messages, not emotion[cite: 32]. [cite_start]Based on your understanding of the audiences, draft the messages in the way to match their expected communication way[cite: 33]. 
* [cite_start]For example, if your audience is a visual learner, prepare your message via data/chart[cite: 34]. 
* [cite_start]If the key stakeholder is an executive and prefers 2 minutes talk, prepare your messages in an extremely simplified way to target what he/she/they wants to know[cite: 35]. 

## Determine the HOW to manage your communication
[cite_start]Communication is not just what we say, but how we deliver it[cite: 38]. [cite_start]On the top, keep the following in the mind before you communicate[cite: 39]:
* [cite_start]Communicate with presence, love, care and passion[cite: 40]. [cite_start]Compassionate and mindful communication is the key skill to help the information transition cover both perspectives in order to reach a conflict resolution based on the understanding[cite: 41]. 
* [cite_start]Understand your audiences' communication styles[cite: 43]. [cite_start]You may talk to other internal stakeholders who know them[cite: 45]. [cite_start]Their LinkedIn profiles and blogs can also give you a hint[cite: 46]. 

[cite_start]There are many ways to assess a person's style[cite: 48]. [cite_start]TILT365 is a tool Red Hat team uses often to identify our personality and strengths by nature[cite: 49]. [cite_start]There are 4 TILT types: Connection, Clarity, Structure and Impact[cite: 50]. [cite_start]At the next level, DISC assessment generates a deep understanding of your natural and adopted behavior styles: Dominance, Influence, Steadiness and Conscientiousness[cite: 52]. 

## In the live communication, here are the best practices:
* [cite_start]**Keep the WHY and the desired outcome in the mind:** Use this as a guideline to lead the conversations so you can focus on what you want from it[cite: 55].
* [cite_start]**Acknowledge presence and concerns:** By saying "I represent my team and am here to help." and "I have the same goal as you which is to solve the problem.", it can quickly build up the connection with the audience[cite: 56, 57].
* [cite_start]**Stay unbiased:** Please respect the audience's opinion during crucial conversations[cite: 59].
* [cite_start]**Use words which can contribute to the situation solution:** It is vital to choose the professional words wisely to help "heal", not to "hurt" [cite: 61][cite_start]; to focus on the "facts", not the "opinion"[cite: 62].
* [cite_start]**Manage your emotions timely:** When you start feeling uncomfortable, take a deep breath and remind yourself that this is about the business, not about me[cite: 64]. [cite_start]If the conversation gets stuck, it is OK to pause by accepting that we are vulnerable, refresh your position and ask what the other end wants, and then reschedule a time to follow up[cite: 66].
* [cite_start]**Master your mindful listening skills:** Listen with your full attention while digesting what the speaker addresses is related to your WHY[cite: 67]. [cite_start]It helps build up a safe space if you allow others to speak freely without judgment or interruption[cite: 68].
* [cite_start]**Read the room:** Be mindful about the audiences' reaction and recognize the turning point when the conversation gets crucial[cite: 69]. [cite_start]It is the time when you need to adjust your communication approach[cite: 70].
* [cite_start]**Note down all the agreed points:** Capture all the key agreements including the action items[cite: 71]. [cite_start]Finalize and communicate the call summary timely[cite: 72].

## Summary
[cite_start]The WHY behind the communication seldom changes along the situation management life cycle[cite: 75]. [cite_start]It is like the paint brush in your hand when drawing your artwork[cite: 76]. [cite_start]To come out with a perfect artwork, each step which is driven by your brush should connect to that WHY[cite: 78]. [cite_start]Please carry these practices when exploring your own way toward becoming a more effective communicator, both personally and professionally[cite: 79].
